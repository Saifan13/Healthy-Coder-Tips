Strengthen Your Body

1- Let's get physical

You don't have to take your kayak out onto the lake during your lunch break, but getting regular exercise is key to being a healthy coder.

Take short breaks throughout your day to get up from your desk and stretch your muscles. And when you sit back down, focus on maintaining good posture at your desk.

During your lunch break, take a walk outside to get a change of scenery and improve your circulation. This will help to improve your focus when you get back to your coding projects.



2- Chow down on a carrot

Pack a healthy lunch or take a brisk walk to a local shop or grocery store to pick up a healthy meal.

Choose fresh, healthy ingredients and stay away from processed foods. Soup and sandwich or a salad with a bunch of tasty toppings always hits the spot for energy and nutrition.

Keep healthy snacks at your desk within easy reach. Carrot sticks or sliced apples with peanut butter are great. Many coders enjoy trail-mix with raisins, nuts and seeds. Make your own recipe to match your tastes.



3- Water, water everywhere

In Colorado's dry climate, it can be difficult to stay hydrated throughout the workday.

That's why it's so important to be sure to drink plenty of water while you're busy coding.

When choosing a beverage, remember that many caffeinated beverages like coffee and soda will actually dehydrate your mind and body.

Make water your go-to beverage and limit your intake of drinks that sap your vitality.
